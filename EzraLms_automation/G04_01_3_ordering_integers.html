What is Ordering Integers?

Ordering integers means putting numbers in a sequence — either from smallest to largest or from largest to smallest.

↑
Ascending
Smallest → Largest
↓
Descending
Largest → Smallest

Key Idea

🔑 On a number line, numbers to the RIGHT are GREATER. Numbers to the LEFT are SMALLER.

Steps to Order Integers

1
Find each number's position on the number line
2
Identify negative numbers (left of zero) and positive numbers (right of zero)
3
For ascending: start from the leftmost (most negative), move right
4
For descending: start from the rightmost (largest positive), move left
Using the Number Line

A number line helps us see the order of integers clearly. Numbers increase from left to right!

Number Line
← Negative (smaller) Positive (greater) →

Ordering Rules

📍Numbers on the RIGHT are always GREATER than numbers on the LEFT
❄️For negative numbers: the one closer to ZERO is GREATER (-2 > -5)
☀️Every positive is greater than zero. Every negative is less than zero.
Examples

Ascending Order (Smallest to Largest)

-5 < -2 < 3 < 7

-5, -2, 3, 7 → From most negative to most positive

Descending Order (Largest to Smallest)

8 > 4 > -1 > -6

8, 4, -1, -6 → From largest positive to most negative

Mixed Positive and Negative

-7 < -3 < 0 < 5

Always: negative numbers < zero < positive numbers

🏆 Temperature Ranking

Four cities had these temperatures: -5°C, 8°C, -12°C, 3°C. In ascending order: -12°C < -5°C < 3°C < 8°C. The coldest city had the lowest temperature!
